How It Began

From a Home Kitchen to Surat's Favourite Bakery

Golden Crust Bakery began in a small apartment kitchen in Adajan, Surat. Priya Mehta had just returned from a 3-month baking apprenticeship in Pune and was determined to bring authentic artisan bread to her hometown — a city better known for its sweets than its breads.

She began delivering sourdough loaves to her neighbors on weekends. Within three months, the demand outgrew her home oven. Her husband Rajan quit his job in finance and together they signed a lease on a tiny shop on Bakers Lane — a name they considered the perfect omen.

Today, Golden Crust employs 12 bakers and serves hundreds of families and businesses across Surat every week. But the philosophy has never changed: real ingredients, real time, real love.

What We Stand For

Our Values

🌿

Natural Ingredients

We never use artificial preservatives, colors, or flavors. Every ingredient is sourced from trusted local suppliers.

⏰

Slow Fermentation

Our sourdoughs ferment for 18–24 hours. Good bread can't be rushed — and we never try to.

🤝

Community First

We source locally, employ locally, and give back to the Surat community through our weekend free-bread program for low-income families.

♻️

Zero Waste

Leftover bread is converted into breadcrumbs, croutons, or donated — we aim for zero food waste every day.
